Abstract

The Eukaryote domain reflects a wide variety of organisms that all spanned from a common eukaryotic ancestor. Over 8 million eukaryotic species have risen since the branching of eukaryotes from archaea around 2 billion years ago. Despite billions of years of evolution, many proteins have been found to be well-conserved across many different eukaryotic lineages. The deep conservation of these proteins emphasizes the importance and ubiquitous nature of their roles in eukaryotes. CFMS (co-fractionation mass spectrometry) in turn outputs elution profiles for proteins. Using results from CFMS, we can predict which protein families are most similar to each other and are likely to form protein complexes that are conserved across different eukaryotic organisms. To this end, we used AlphaFold to predict the feasibility of possible predicted protein complexes across different eukaryote species.
