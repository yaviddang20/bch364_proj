Discusion

Looking at the pIDDT and observing the different protein complex models created by AlphaFold are ways to validate the validity of a protein complex. Protein complexes with too low pIDDTs throughout the sequence are unlikely to form a complex. An ideal pIDDT curve would be consistently near the top without many or any drops in pIDDT at all.

AlphaFold generates 5 models that are based on different training parameters. Similarity between model structures is a good indicator that the protein complex predicted by AlphaFold is likely to be correct, while the opposite is true for models of the same complex that differ more in structure. Another way to differentiate bad models from good models is the presence of disordered regions. Disordered regions may indicate that AlphaFold may not be confident in determining the structure of that region.

Example of a protein complex containing disordered regions predicted by AlphaFold (Yeast KOG1092-KOG2806).

All three organisms shown here share the KOG3433-KOG4603 complex. The MND1-HOP2 complex structure has been experimentally determined in Giardia lamblia by x-ray crystallography. Here we can see just by looking at the 3d structures that AlphaFold predicted very similar structures of the KOG3433-KOG4603 complex for the mouse, yeast, and algae species. This signifies that this complex is deeply conserved across different eukaryotes, spanning both unicellular and multicellular organisms, and reflects the importance and ubiquitous nature of the complex.
